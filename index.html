06  ·  Research & Insights

The persona gap — who the site is built for vs. who is actually using it.

Site is Designed For
"Financial Expert"
  • Understands "Flexi Prime," "CIBIL," "LAP," "MSME"
  • Desktop or high-end smartphone user
  • Will scroll and explore independently
  • Researches rates and APR before deciding
  • English-primary language comfort
Actual User Is
"First-Time Loan Seeker"
  • Knows: "Home Loan," "Car Loan," "Shop Loan"
  • Mid-range Android, 4–5" screen, 4G / intermittent data
  • Exits if confused within the first 10 seconds
  • Trust triggers: brand name, local branch, WhatsApp
  • Prefers Hindi / Regional — English is secondary
Heuristic Finding 01
Paradox of Choice (Hick's Law)
100+ menu links create decision fatigue. In a financial context, this directly correlates to drop-off rather than deliberation. More options = longer decision time = higher abandonment.
Heuristic Finding 02
Minimum Viable Information
Tier 2/3 users scan for three numbers: Interest Rate, Maximum Amount, Monthly EMI. All three are currently buried in different sections of a long-scroll page.
Heuristic Finding 03
Trust Before Conversion
For this demographic, trust is the first conversion hurdle — not product features. The current journey presents features before trust. This inverts the correct psychological sequence.
Heuristic Finding 04
Progressive Disclosure Required
The site presents all information upfront. The correct model: show the minimum to build interest → reveal detail only when the user actively requests it.

08D  ·  Complete Site Audit — Commercial Vehicle Loan

poonawallafincorp.com/commercial-vehicle-loan

Critical Gap
No EMI Calculator
Users cannot calculate affordability on the page. Must call or visit a branch to understand loan cost — a massive friction point for Tier 2/3 transport business owners.
Critical Gap
No Interest Rate Displayed
Interest rate is not displayed anywhere on the page. This is both a major trust gap and a potential disclosure compliance issue.
Critical Gap
No Eligibility Details
Users have no way to self-qualify on the page. All eligibility queries require contacting support — adding friction and support cost.
Visual Context Issues
  • Abstract financial imagery — no actual vehicle photos. Tier 2/3 transport owners (truckers, taxi operators, tempo drivers) cannot see themselves reflected in the page.
  • No vehicle category distinction — trucks, commercial tempos, buses, and mini-vans all have different loan structures but are presented identically.
  • Document list is text-only. Users don't know which documents are mandatory vs. optional. Suggested fix: add photo-style icons (RC Book, Aadhaar, Bank Statement).

10  ·  Compliance & Risk

In financial services, UX failures are also compliance failures.

Critical
Regulatory Disclosure
The Rates & Charges grid is not responsive — "Conditions Apply" text is hidden on mobile, creating a compliance exposure for undisclosed terms.
High
Accessibility (WCAG)
Color contrast issues on CTAs must be resolved to meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards. Body text below 16px in multiple sections fails minimum mobile readability requirements.
Additional Accessibility Gaps
  • Color-coding used for status indicators — not accessible to color-vision-impaired users
  • No alt-text on carousel images — screen reader inaccessible
  • High contrast mode not tested or supported
  • No vernacular support — excludes non-English speaking Tier 2/3 users entirely
Missing CV Loan Disclosures
  • Interest rate not displayed on Commercial Vehicle Loan page — potential disclosure non-compliance
  • No eligibility criteria published — forces offline interaction which adds compliance complexity
  • No processing fee disclosure on the CV Loan page
